EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

THIS REPORT DETAILS THE DESIGN PROCESS OF A VERTICAL TOWER HYDROPONIC DRIP SYSTEM FOR GROWING PERENNIAL HERBS. THE VERTICAL TOWER SYSTEM AIMS TO MAKE HOMEGROWN HERBS ACCESSIBLE IN SMALL NYC APARTMENTS WITHOUT AVAILABLE OUTDOOR GROWING SPACE, PROVIDING NYC RESIDENTS WITH DIRECT ACCESS TO A LOCAL FOOD SOURCE. THE VERTICAL TOWER DESIGN COULD ALSO BE DUPLICATED AND ADAPTED INTO A LARGER, MODULAR SYSTEM TO ALLOW FOR INCREASED YIELDS IN LARGER GROWING SPACES.

INSPIRATION

HAVING GROWN UP WITH ACCESS TO FRESH HOMEGROWN PRODUCE AND COMING FROM A PROFESSIONAL BACKGROUND IN ORGANIC AGRICULTURE, I CHERISH THE PRIVILEGE OF HAVING ACCESS TO FRESH, LOCALLY-GROWN PRODUCE. LACK OF LAND ACCESS AND SCALE OF HOUSING ACCOMMODATIONS ARE AMONG THE MANY PROHIBITIVE FACTORS TO GROWING ONE’S OWN FOOD IN NEW YORK CITY. THIS VERTICAL TOWER DESIGN AIMS TO OPTIMIZE GROWING CAPACITY WHILE MINIMIZING SQUARE FOOTAGE, ENABLING URBAN DWELLERS TO GROW THEIR OWN PRODUCE. THIS SYSTEM IS OPTIMIZED TO GROW PERENNIAL HERBS TO PROVIDE LONG-TERM ACCESS WHILE MINIMIZING WASTE AND MAINTENANCE COSTS, BUT IS EASILY ADAPTED OR SCALED TO ACCOMMODATE OTHER TYPES OF CROPS.

PROBLEM

LIMITED LAND ACCESS AND SMALL APARTMENTS PRESENT MAJOR BARRIERS TO GROWING ONE’S OWN FOOD IN NEW YORK CITY, RESULTING IN A HIGHLY DELOCALIZED FOOD SYSTEM. CONSUMERS ARE DISCONNECTED FROM THEIR FOOD SOURCES, AND FRESH PRODUCE IS WIDELY INACCESSIBLE.

KEY CONSIDERATIONS

EARLY IN THE DESIGN PROCESS, I IDENTIFIED THE FOLLOWING AS PRIORITIES FOR THE DESIGN OF MY HYDROPONICS SYSTEM: LOWEST RESOURCE EXTRACTION, AESTHETICS OF THE SYSTEM, AND LOW MAINTENANCE. AS THIS SYSTEM IS DESIGNED PRIMARILY FOR IMPLENTATION IN PRIVATE RESIDENCES, IT WAS IMPORTANT FOR ME TO CONSIDER THE SYSTEM'S RESOURCE AND MAINTENANCE NEEDS, AS WELL AS VISUAL IMPACT ON THE SPACE IN WHICH IT IS BUILT. I ALSO CONSIDERED EASE OF ASSEMBLY, MODULARITY, AND ABILITY TO BREAK DOWN, MOVE, AND REASSEMBLE THE SYSTEM. OF COURSE, SAFETY CONCERNS AND PROPER EQUIPMENT MAINTENAINCE WERE KEY DESIGN CONSIDERATIONS.

RECOMMENDATIONS

SAFETY RECOMMENDATIONS INCLUDE THOROUGH ASSESSMENT OF THE AREA IN WHICH THE SYSTEM IS TO BE BUILT, ESPECIALLY THE IDENTIFICATION OF POTENTIAL ELECTRICAL AND CHEMICAL HAZARDS, AND ROUTINE MAINTENAINCE IN ACCORDANCE WITH THE PROCEDURES DETAILED IN THE STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURES DOCUMENT.

MANY DESIGN ELEMENTS OF THIS SYSTEM ARE ADAPTABLE BASED ON INDIVIDUAL NEEDS AND DESIRES; IF WORKING WITH DIFFERENT MATERIALS THAN THOSE IDENTIFIED IN THE MATERIALS LIST, ENSURE THAT THEY ARE FOOD SAFE AND DURABLE.

RATIONALE

THIS SYSTEM IS DESIGNED PRIMARILY FOR HOUSEHOLD USE. WHILE SAFETY PRECAUTIONS ARE PARAMOUNT AND SHOULD BE PRIORITIZED, DESIGN CONSIDERATIONS ARE PERSONAL AND SHOULD REFLECT THE NEEDS OF THE INDIVIDUAL.

PROJECT ANALYSIS

THE PRIMARY CHALLENGE FOR THE DESIGN OF THIS HYDROPONIC SYSTEM IS ACCESS TO GROWING SPACE. GROWING VERTICALLY MINIMIZES THE SYSTEM'S FOOTPRINT WHILE STILL ALLOWING FOR A SIZABLE AND ADAPTABLE GROW CAPACITY. ADDITIONALLY, WORKING WITHIN THE CONSTRAINT OF A $500 BUDGET MEANT SELECTING MATERIALS THAT ARE AFFORDABLE AND ACCESSIBLE. PART OF THE DESIGN'S ACCESSIBILITY AND SUSTAINABILITY IS REFLECTED IN ITS EASE OF MAINTENAINCE; THIS SYSTEM IS DESIGNED AS A HOUSEHOLD HYDROPONICS SOLUTION, AND THEREFORE GENERAL MAINTENANCE IS QUITE STRAIGHTFORWARD (ROUTINE RESERVOIR REFILL AND CLEANING).

THE VERTICAL TOWER SYSTEM'S GREATEST STRENGTH IS ITS SIMPLICITY AND ADAPTABILITY. AS A RELATIVELY SMALL, FREE-STANDING SYSTEM, IT IS EASY TO RELOCATE WITHOUT DISTURBING THE PLANTS IN THE SYSTEM. THE NUMBER OF GROW BUCKETS AND DISTANCE BETWEEN BUCKETS IS FULLY CUSTOMIZABLE AND ADAPTABLE WITHIN THE SYSTEM, ALLOWING FOR AN ARRAY OF PLANTS TO THRIVE.

THE SYSTEM'S SIMPLICITY DOES ALSO CREATE SOME LIMITATIONS; BECAUSE THE GROW POTS SHARE THE SAME WATER AND NUTRIENT SOLUTION, EACH SYSTEL IS LIMITED TO GROWING CROPS WITH THE SAME OR VERY SIMILAR NUTRITIONAL AND ENVIRONMENTAL CONDITIONS.

CHALLENGES

THE MAIN CHALLENGE FOR THIS DESIGN WAS DETERMINING THE BEST SYSTEM TO OPTIMIZE VERTICAL GROWING SPACE. THE SYSTEM IS DESIGNED TO BE PLACED IN FRONT OF A WINDOW, WHICH ELIMINATED THE POTENTIAL FOR WALL-MOUNTED EQUIPMENT.

SOLUTIONS

I WAS ABLE TO DETERMINE THE IDEAL SYSTEM FOR MY GROWING SPACE BY CONDUCTING THOROUGH RESEARCH. I RELIED HEAVILY ON YOUTUBE VIDEOS TO COMPARE DIFFERENT HYDROPONICS SYSTEMS AND GENERATE IDEAS FOR MY OWN SYSTEM DESIGN. THE RESULT IS A STURDY, FREE-STANDING TOWER SYSTEM THAT IS EASY TO MOVE AND ADAPT.

CONCLUSION

THE FLOOD AND DRAIN HYDROPONIC TOWER SYSTEM THAT I DESIGNED DID MEET MY GOALS OF MINIMIZING RESOURCE USAGE AND BEING RELATIVELY LOW-MAINTENANCE. CHOOSING TO GROW HERBS THAT CAN BE KEPT IN THE SYSTEM AND HARVESTED MANY TIMES REDUCES WASTE AND PROVIDES LONGTERM ACCESS TO FRESH HERBS FROM A SINGLE GROW CYCLE. HOWEVER, IN ORDER TO BETTER MEET MY GOALS OF AESTHETICS AND ADAPTABILITY, I WOULD CONSIDER CHOOSING A DIFFERENT LOCATION AND OPTING FOR A WALL-MOUNTED SYSTEM.

IT IS IMPORTANT WHEN REPLICATING THIS SYSTEM TO CONDUCT THOROUGH RESEARCH ON THE NEEDS OF THE SPECIFIC CROPS ONE WISHES TO GROW, AND TO ADAPT THE SYSTEM AS NEEDED. FOR EXAMPLE, ONE MAY NEED TO REDUCE THE NUMBER OF GROW BUCKETS AND INCREASE THE BUCKET SPACING IN ORDER TO SUCCESSFULLY GROW LARGER, FRUITING CROPS.

IN CARRYING OUT THIS DESIGN PROJECT, I HAVE LEARNED THAT GROWING FOOD IN A NEW YORK CITY APARTMENT IS MUCH MORE ACHIEVABLE THAN I THOUGHT, ESPECIALLY COMING FROM A BACKGROUND IN SOIL-BASED AGRICULTURE. HYDROPONIC GROWING REQUIRES DILIGENT AND PRECISE MONITORING AND UPKEEP, BUT THIS DESIGN PROCESS HAS HIGHLIGHTED THAT HOUSEHOLD GROWING CAN BE RESOURCE-, COST-, AND SPACE-EFFICIENT.
