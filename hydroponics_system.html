EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

THIS REPORT DETAILS THE DESIGN PROCESS OF A VERTICAL TOWER HYDROPONIC DRIP SYSTEM FOR GROWING PERENNIAL HERBS. THE VERTICAL TOWER SYSTEM AIMS TO MAKE HOMEGROWN HERBS ACCESSIBLE IN SMALL NYC APARTMENTS WITHOUT AVAILABLE OUTDOOR GROWING SPACE, PROVIDING NYC RESIDENTS WITH DIRECT ACCESS TO A LOCAL FOOD SOURCE. THE VERTICAL TOWER DESIGN COULD ALSO BE DUPLICATED AND ADAPTED INTO A LARGER, MODULAR SYSTEM TO ALLOW FOR INCREASED YIELDS IN LARGER GROWING SPACES.

PROJECT OVERVIEW

INSPIRATION

HAVING GROWN UP WITH ACCESS TO FRESH HOMEGROWN PRODUCE AND COMING FROM A PROFESSIONAL BACKGROUND IN ORGANIC AGRICULTURE, I CHERISH THE PRIVILEGE OF HAVING ACCESS TO FRESH, LOCALLY-GROWN PRODUCE. LACK OF LAND ACCESS AND SCALE OF HOUSING ACCOMMODATIONS ARE AMONG THE MANY PROHIBITIVE FACTORS TO GROWING ONE’S OWN FOOD IN NEW YORK CITY. THIS VERTICAL TOWER DESIGN AIMS TO OPTIMIZE GROWING CAPACITY WHILE MINIMIZING SQUARE FOOTAGE, ENABLING URBAN DWELLERS TO GROW THEIR OWN PRODUCE. THIS SYSTEM IS OPTIMIZED TO GROW PERENNIAL HERBS TO PROVIDE LONG-TERM ACCESS WHILE MINIMIZING WASTE AND MAINTENANCE COSTS, BUT IS EASILY ADAPTED OR SCALED TO ACCOMMODATE OTHER TYPES OF CROPS.

PROBLEM

LIMITED LAND ACCESS AND SMALL APARTMENTS PRESENT MAJOR BARRIERS TO GROWING ONE’S OWN FOOD IN NEW YORK CITY, RESULTING IN A HIGHLY DELOCALIZED FOOD SYSTEM. CONSUMERS ARE DISCONNECTED FROM THEIR FOOD SOURCES, AND FRESH PRODUCE IS WIDELY INACCESSIBLE.

PROJECT TIMELINE: AUGUST 26, 2024-OCTOBER 31, 2024
